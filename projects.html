2023 - 2024

Morphing Robotic Limb for Amphibious Robot

Published at IEEE RoboSoft 2024 | Second Author

Designed an internal pneumatic actuator for an amphibious robotic limb that reduced morphing energy by 99% and actuation pressure by 93% compared to previous designs. The improved iteration featured a Kirigami layer jamming system with internal pneumatic actuator, achieving significant improvements in hydrodynamics with a 53% reduction in profile thickness.

Characterized actuator performance through comprehensive Instron compression and three-point bending tests across multiple actuation states. Processed experimental data in MATLAB to generate figures for publication. Redesigned and 3D printed the robot's modular shell using SolidWorks surface modeling to improve field serviceability.

Publication: J. Sun, B. Lin, et al., "Performance Enhancement of a Morphing Limb for an Amphibious Robotic Turtle," IEEE RoboSoft 2024.

SolidWorks MATLAB Instron Testing 3D Printing Pneumatic Systems Research
